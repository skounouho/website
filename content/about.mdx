# About

I'm Senou. I build things under uncertainty.

Right now that means [Akara Markets](https://akaramarkets.com) — a startup I co-founded in January 2026 delivering courtside intelligence for sports prediction markets. I lead product and write code across a shared React / React Native frontend and a Python backend handling real-time sports data under live-game latency.

Before New York, four years at Duke studying mechanical engineering with a math minor, mostly in front of simulations. The research thread ran through the Uncertainty Quantification in Computational Mechanics Lab, with Prof. Johann Guilleminot: stochastic reduced-order models for molecular dynamics, and a sensitivity-analysis framework for model-choice uncertainty — two papers in *Computer Methods in Applied Mechanics and Engineering*. Summers took the same questions elsewhere. Sandia's Center for Integrated Nanotechnologies for polymer networks and metal films. Argonne for additive-manufacturing defect modeling, which shipped as a DOE-released tool called COBRA.

Outside the math: teaching (fluid mechanics, data structures, a student-taught house course on energy and climate investing, another on philanthropy in Durham), reporting for *The Chronicle*, and jazz — which is its own kind of improvisation under uncertainty.
